🔬 What Data are you viewing?

JWST records physics measurements in **FITS format**—numerical data matrices that track incoming photon counts pixel by pixel. Because infrared radiation is completely invisible to human eyes, raw images initially appear blank.

⚙️ How are Images generated?

Deep space emissions are incredibly faint. The server evaluates the raw matrix on-the-fly, executing a non-linear **Logarithmic Stretch** function. This compresses extreme contrast boundaries, pulling invisible dust filaments out of noisy baselines.

🛰️ Hardware Lens & Chromatic Decoder Matrix

False-color assignments translate invisible light waves into meaningful dimensions based on physical perspective. By toggling filters, you are slicing through deep space layer by layer to isolate different physical depths and states of matter:

🔴 Reds & Oranges | The Cosmic Shoreline 0.6µm - 2.0µm

Spatial Perspective: Foreground Shells & Outlines This palette isolates high-energy waves bouncing directly off the outermost skins and geometric boundaries of matter. Because these waves are short, they cannot penetrate dense cosmic walls; instead, they trace the definitive structural boundaries holding gas matrices together. Use this view to study sharp galaxy profiles, star silhouettes, and kinetic plasma outlines sitting in front of the deeper space fog. Primary Instruments: NIRCam / NIRISS

🟢 Greens & Teals | The Midground Fog 2.0µm - 5.0µm

Spatial Perspective: Interstellar Lanes & Volumetric Clouds This palette illuminates the vast mid-ground gaps drifting between foreground stars and the deep background. These mid-range waves match the exact scale of complex organic molecules, capturing light weaving through internal spaces. Think of bright galaxy cores as flashlights in a dark room; this channel lights up the sweeping paths of organic hydrocarbon soot floating between the systems. Primary Instruments: NIRISS / NIRCam

🔵 Blues & Violets | Deep Thermal Cores 5.0µm - 28.3µm

Spatial Perspective: Absolute Deep Background & Core Interiors Long, slow mid-infrared waves pass completely through foreground structures and pierce straight through midground green fog, escaping from the coldest, most deeply buried environments. Foreground stars fade to black here, unmasking everything hidden behind the curtains: cold comet rings, thermal planetary dust lines, and embryonic baby stars incubating inside the absolute center of pitch-black nebulae. Primary Instrument: MIRI
